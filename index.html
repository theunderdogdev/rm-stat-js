Test - Z-Test Anova
What is anova?
  • Statistical technique to test difference.
  • Tests differences between the means of 3 or more independent groups.
  • Types - 1 way, 2 way.
  • Test Statistic - F-Test.
One Way Anova
  • A one-way ANOVA is a statistical test used to determine the effect of one independent variable on multiple groups or classes
  • Simple example would be comparison of Income w.r.t Education Level (Bachelors, Masters, Doctoral).
Two Way Anova
  • A two-way ANOVA is a statistical test used to determine the effect of two independent variables on a continuous outcome variable.
  • Simple example would be influence of multiple detergents in Cold, Warm, Hot water.
What is z-test?
  • Statistical test to examine if the sample mean is different from population mean.
  • Standard Deviation should be provided.
  • Types - 1 Sampled, 2 Sampled
  • Test Statistic - z-test
One Sampled Z-Test
  • One sample z-test, tests if the sample mean is different from the population mean
  • Example: Effect of vaccination on the anger level of pets whether the anger level is different from the original population.
Two Sampled Z-Test
  • Two sampled z-test, tests if the sample means of two independent categories are different.
  • Example: Testing whether there is a significant difference in IQ levels of individuals in two different cities.
